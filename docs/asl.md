# ASL

## What is ASL?

ASL means **Average Sentence Length**. In other words, it is the average number of words per sentence in a text. It is a classic indicator of readability and clarity, used in education, stylometric analysis, and professional writing.

The ASL score is not sophisticated, but it gives useful information about writing style: a text with short sentences is usually easier to read and understand; one with long sentences may seem elevated, but can become tiring.

## How is ASL calculated?

The formula is very simple:

$$
ASL = \frac{\text{total number of words}}{\text{total number of sentences}}
$$

Examples:

- A text with 120 words and 10 sentences -> ASL = 120 / 10 = 12
- A text with 250 words and 25 sentences -> ASL = 10

Sentences are treated as sequences delimited by `.`, `?`, and `!`.

## How do we interpret the ASL score?

In general, a lower ASL score indicates a more accessible style, while a higher one can suggest complexity, but also difficulty. The readability limit for a general audience is around **15-17 words per sentence**.

| ASL | Average Length | Text Type | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 8 | Extremely short | Chat messages, slogans, headlines | Very brief copy |
| 8-10 | Short | Instructions, announcements, social posts | Informational snippets |
| 11-13 | Accessible | Press articles, clear blog posts | General online writing |
| 14-16 | Medium | Editorials, simple essays, popular books | Balanced prose |
| 17-19 | Moderately long | Political news, economic analysis | Denser argumentation |
| 20-22 | Complex | Introductory literary or academic texts | Concept-heavy writing |
| 23-25 | Very long | Studies, scientific papers | Formal analytical prose |
| > 25 | Extremely long | Legal language, archaic texts | Hard-to-read formal text |

## Limits and usefulness

ASL does not measure vocabulary, tone, or emotional content, but it says a lot about writing style. It is an essential metric for:

- clear writing
- simplifying texts for a broad audience
- comparative analysis between authors or genres

It is also one of the components of the **LIX** score, but it can be used separately as a quick diagnostic tool.

## Conclusion

ASL is perhaps the most direct indicator of written clarity: the shorter the sentences, the easier the text is to read. However, simplicity should not be confused with superficiality - short sentences can express complex ideas, and long sentences may contain very little.
