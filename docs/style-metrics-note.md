# How to read the style metrics scores

These metrics are signals more than they are grades. They do not decide whether an article is good, true, useful, funny, elegant, or worth publishing.

Their purpose is to show tendencies: repetition, density, rhythm, readability, lexical variety, and tone. A good article may score high on difficulty. A weak article may score well on readability. The numbers are useful only when read together with the actual text.

I use them as editorial cues: places where I may want to reread, tighten, split, vary, simplify, enrich, or deliberately leave things exactly as they are.
